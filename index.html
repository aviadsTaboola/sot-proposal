The Solution: Skeleton-of-Thought (SoT)

SoT is a two-step method inspired by how humans write: plan first, then execute. We call this the "Architect & Builder" approach, which breaks a large, slow task into smaller, parallelizable steps.

Data-Driven Decisions

Our recommendation is based on the paper's rigorous evaluation. This section provides a deeper look at the data for the algorithm team, building confidence in the proposed solution.

A Note on Metrics: The quality evaluations cited from the paper use two LLM-based frameworks, FastChat and LLMZoo. In both, a powerful LLM (like GPT-4) acts as a judge, comparing the SoT-generated answer against the standard one to determine a "win," "tie," or "loss."

This chart plots models based on speed-up and quality. The top-right is ideal. Notably, across all tests, SoT's answers were judged as equal or better in over 70% of cases, highlighting a low-risk path to quality gains. Hover over points for details.

Proposed Next Steps

We propose a phased approach to de-risk the project and ensure a smooth, data-driven rollout, moving from a small-scale test to full integration.

Phase 1: Proof of Concept

Build a prototype using StableVicuna-13B and test on a benchmark of 20 articles. Measure latency, quality, and cost.
